About

This is a collection of experiments on transformer training dynamics, optimizer design, and language model architecture. Most of the ideas didn't work. The measurements are real.

Setup

  • Toy model: 5M param custom GPT, synthetic bigram data
  • Real model: 73M param nanochat (Karpathy), FineWeb-Edu
  • MuZero LM: 27M param transformer + world model, TinyStories
  • Hardware: M4 Mac Mini 24GB (all experiments except MuZero)
  • MuZero hardware: RunPod L40S (single GPU, ~4 hours training)
  • Optimizer: Muon + AdamW (nanochat default)

Every number comes from an actual experiment. No theory-only claims. All code and raw results are reproducible.

Compute limitations: Running on a Mac Mini means small batch sizes (8-64), short training runs (1000-5000 steps), and no multi-GPU parallelism. Some findings may not transfer to proper scale. The gradient rank measurements in particular are batch-size sensitive — larger batches produce cleaner, lower-rank gradient signals.

Why publish failures

ML research has a publication bias toward things that work. But knowing what doesn't work — and exactly why — is just as valuable. If these notes save someone else from spending weeks on SpectraMuon or cross-batch curvature, they've done their job.
